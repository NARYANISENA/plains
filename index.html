The Indian Plains

The plains of India are fertile areas of land between the Indus and Ganga rivers. They are suitable for agriculture and other activities. There are many plains like Tarai plains , Bhangar , Delta plains and Rajasthan plains.

Key features include:

  • Bhur: A raised land area along the Ganga River's banks. It is generated by the buildup of wind-blown sands during the hot, dry months.
  • Unique flora and fauna adapted to harsh conditions.
  • Flat topography: The plains have a very flat surface with little variation in relief.
  • Alluvial deposits: The plains are made up of alluvial deposits from the rivers, which can be up to 2,000 meters deep.
  • Fertile soil: The plains are ideal for farming and cultivation due to their fertile soil.

The Indian plains are a vital part of India, Nepal, and Bangladesh. The region is known as a cradle of civilization and is home to many people.
