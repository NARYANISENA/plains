The Indian Plains

The Indian Plains are vast stretches of flatlands that form one of the most fertile regions of the country. They are primarily divided into three sections: the Northern Plains, the Coastal Plains, and the Indo-Gangetic Plains.

Northern Plains

The Northern Plains are formed by the deposition of alluvial soil brought down by rivers like the Ganga, Brahmaputra, and Yamuna. Key features include:

  • Extremely fertile soil, ideal for agriculture.
  • Dense population due to favorable living conditions.
  • Home to major cities like Delhi, Kolkata, and Lucknow.

Coastal Plains

The Coastal Plains stretch along the eastern and western coasts of India. Key features include:

  • Known for ports like Mumbai, Chennai, and Kochi.
  • Rich in marine resources and coconut plantations.
  • Divided into the Eastern Coastal Plains and Western Coastal Plains.

Indo-Gangetic Plains

The Indo-Gangetic Plains are a part of the Northern Plains and are known for their:

  • High agricultural productivity.
  • Rivers like the Ganga, Yamuna, and their tributaries.
  • Rich cultural heritage and historical significance.
